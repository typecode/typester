Another WYSIWYG? But why?

This is the question we asked ourselves as well before setting out to write this package. With over 576 results when searching for "wysiwyg" on npmjs.com what could possibly possess us to take on the challenge of writing another from scratch?

To be honest: None of the ones we looked at and played with did the trick for us. Either they were too stringent in their layout, or too complex in their execution, or they are in some way tainted by their heavy reliance on the browser execCommand() output which, for all its power, is slave to the various browser quirks and oddities that cause malformed and dirty HTML output.

We are, as most, quite taken by the simplicity of the editor developed by the team at Medium.com for their authoring tools. And so we began by using the medium-editor developed by yabwe. And it served use well for the most part, and to yabwe we tip our hat. The work that the contributors did on that package was incredible.

That said, however, we still found ourselves wanting. The medium-editor package relied too heavily on the execCommand() output as well as having some UI issues that we weren't able to rectify via the API it exposed, and we weren't willing to hack into their code to get what we wanted.

And what did we want? Clean and predictable HTML regardless of the browser used to input the content. And so after much deliberation we began crafting our own solution.

A solution that would:
Take user input → process and standardize it → sanitize it → return awesome HTML

All while refining the UI experience.

And that is a very brief history, and explanation, of why we invested our time and energy into producing, what we feel is, a pretty damn good WYSIWYG that we like to call: Typester
